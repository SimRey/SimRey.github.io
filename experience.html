University of Waterloo

January 2023 - Present

During my tenure as a graduate student, I undertook a dynamic role as a Teaching Assistant for various courses while actively pursuing my research project. My research work centered around the application of Reinforcement Learning techniques to address complex challenges within Chemical Engineering, particularly focusing on Process Systems Engineering (PSE).

My research objectives involves the integration of these advanced agents with sophisticated modeling suites like ASPEN Plus. These agents, characterized by their intricate adaptations of conventional algorithms, include the development of a hybrid Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) agent. This innovative agent facilitated seamless transitions between continuous and discrete action selections, further enhanced by an additional masked discrete selection.

Research assistat

Universidad Iberoamericana (IBERO)

January 2022 - July 2022

Throughout my academic journey, I actively contributed to the creation of a hybrid platform, seamlessly integrating Python with the chemical engineering simulation suite ASPEN. This innovative platform was designed to facilitate real-time optimization, a capability that proved invaluable in various applications. Notably, I applied this platform to optimize the composition of a working fluid within an Organic Rankine Cycle (ORC) using advanced genetic algorithms.

Some of the packages and libraries used in this project were:

  • Pymoo: A Python library featuring pre-built genetic algorithms, prominently NSGA-II.
  • NumPy: A fundamental package for numerical computations in Python, facilitating array operations and mathematical functions.
  • Pandas: A versatile data manipulation and analysis library for Python, offering data structures and tools for efficient data handling.
  • Win32 COM: A technology used to establish connections with applications, such as ASPEN, for seamless interaction and automation.
